The “safe journey” app for Nepali migrant workers

The Shuvayatra mobile app makes the process of labor migration safer and more secure.


Information migrants need to stay safe

Every day, close to 2000 Nepalis leave Kathmandu for temporary jobs in the Gulf Countries and East Asia. Many of these young workers struggle to obtain information on the complex process of migration and its many pitfalls, leaving them vulnerable to labor exploitation, human trafficking, and financial distress.

With Nepal's mobile phone penetration already over 100%, we believe mobile devices hold tremendous potential to help migrants stay safe. The Shuvayatra mobile app connects users to an ever-expanding trove of multimedia content supplied by a consortium of experts, advocates, and peers. Migrants can access quick capsules of relevant information on a range of topics, including:

  • Labor rights and working conditions abroad
  • Work permits and the application process
  • Local do’s and don’ts in Saudi Arabia, Malaysia, and Qatar
  • Unique content specifically for women

Local knowledge

Shuvayatra’s live streaming model gives users a dynamic link to timely, relevant information on the migra­tion process, all in local Nepali languages.

Trusted information

Our partners bring decades of experience working on migration issues in Nepal, and we make sure all of Shuvayatra’s content is accurate and helpful to migrants.

Global network

Through its in-app social media and sharing features, Shuvayatra puts the combined knowledge of the global Nepali diaspora in the hands of migrant workers.

 

Resources & Data

The Shuvayatra team takes a data- and evidence-driven approach to addressing migration challenges, and we make every effort to ensure our work is valuable to others in our field. Our team members have contributed to several landmark reports on the issues affecting migrant workers in Asia, which have helped leaders in government and civil society serve the migrant population more effectively. In this spirit of openness and collaboration, we will continue to share the data and insights we gain.

Who we are

Shuvayatra has been developed and funded by The Asia Foundation, an international NGO with offices in 18 Asian countries, including a permanent presence in Kathmandu. In addition, further support to Shuvayatra comes from a growing consortium of innovators and social entrepreneurs in Nepal:

The Non-Resident Nepali Association (NRNA) is the largest and most prominent umbrella organization of the Nepalese diaspora abroad. NRNA has been one of the initial partners of the Shuvayatra app, and will continue to assist in outreach and user engagement in MENA countries and in Malaysia.

YoungInnovations is a Nepali technology company that creates innovative and powerful solutions to global development problems. Together with YoungInnovations, The Asia Foundation has conducted user-testing among migrant workers, and YoungInnovations will continue to be involved in application development app-centered technical support.

Sharecast Initiative Nepal is a local organization focused on promoting new media and digital/online community public radio in Nepal. Through Sharecast Initiative, Shuvayatra has partnered with 7 local radio stations to support audio content development and drive local promotion efforts.

Hamro Patro is the company behind the most successful Nepali-language mobile app on the iOS and Google Play app stores. Hamro Patro provides high-level strategic guidance to the project as well as technical support for the integration with some of their features and platforms currently in use by Nepalis living in key MENA destination countries and Malaysia.

Bikas Udhyami is a new social innovation hub led by young Nepali professionals with expertise on labor migration issues. As a Shuvayatra partner, Bikas Udhyami will support written content development and social media engagement and promotion.
